Small Pedal Board

for acoustic guitar

The inspiration for this board stems from it's ability to function without external power.

Nine volt batteries furnish the voltage for the tuner and looping pedal, while phantom power fuels the acoustic preamp. The preamp is the workhorse for a passive accoustic pickup system which allows for playing into a variety of sound systems. If needed two none volt batteies can be used for the preamp, although this scenario is rare. During construction, the oak boards were clamped, glued and brad nailed together. The gaps were incorporated to allow flexible cable routing. The second image shows how easily the 90 degree jacks route underneath the surface.

before assembly the unit was sanded and stained, then the corners were futher darkened. After drying, satin polyurathane was applied.

Large Pedal Board

for electric guitar

This board was built to provide plenty of space for pedals, while hiding cables and power supplies.

It was designed for setting out in the living room, without being an eyesore. Rubber feet were added to the bottom for sure footing.

Before the holes were drilled to route the cables, the possible configurations were tested with templates. The final hole locations allow for two spacious rows, or three if some pedals are turned 90 degrees. As you can see there is plenty of space to be creative in to reach all the foot-switches.

The power transoformer is located under board. The finish is a custom layering of black stain milk paint and three layers of satin polyurethane. Input and output holes on the side were counter sunk, for a flush installation of nickle plated jacks. The connecting jacks are custom cut lengths, soldered with silver for excellent signal quality.

The last image shows a close up of the nickle plated jacks, as well as the master power switch and an EIC outlet.
